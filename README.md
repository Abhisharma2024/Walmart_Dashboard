🎯 Project Overview
This project presents a Walmart Sales Performance Dashboard built in Power BI to analyze sales, profit, and customer trends across categories, states, and time.

The goal was to answer critical business questions:

Which products are driving sales? Which states are most profitable? When does profit peak? How is revenue distributed across categories?

This dashboard is designed for Retail Managers, Business Analysts, and Stakeholders to make data-driven decisions.

📌 Dashboard Breakdown - 5 Powerful Visuals
1️⃣ Sum of Sales by Sub-Category (Horizontal Bar)
Top Performer: Phones - $197K
Storage - $150K, Tables - $119K, Paper - $99K, Supplies - $37K
Insight: Technology sub-categories dominate sales.
2️⃣ Sum of Profit by Month (Column Chart)
Peak Months: October & December - $26K each
Lowest: April - $4K
Trend: Q4 (Sep-Dec) is the most profitable quarter
Insight: Holiday season drives massive profit!
3️⃣ Sum of Sales by State (Column Chart)
#1 Texas - $116K
#2 Washington - $93K
Others: Virginia $33K, Tennessee $26K, Wisconsin $21K
Insight: Texas & Washington contribute >70% sales. Focus marketing expansion there.
4️⃣ Sum of Sales by Day (Area Line Chart)
High volatility: Starts at $66K-$68K (Day 1-3), drops to $34K (Day 6), recovers to $62K (Day 13)
Insight: Beginning & middle of month show sales spikes - ideal for promotions.
5️⃣ Sum of Sales by Category (Donut Chart)
Office Supplies: $643.71K (41.11%) - Leader
Technology: $470.59K (30.05%)
Furniture: $451.51K (28.84%)
Insight: Office Supplies is the revenue backbone despite lower individual price.
💡 Key Business Insights
Area	Finding	Actionable Recommendation
Product Strategy	Phones generate highest sub-category sales	Increase inventory & cross-sell accessories
Seasonality	Oct & Dec profit double than avg months	Launch Q4 festive campaigns
Geography	Texas + Washington = Dominant	Open new distribution centers in TX/WA
Category Mix	Office Supplies 41% of total sales	Bundle Office Supplies with Furniture
Risk	April profit crash to 4K	Audit April - supply chain or low demand?
